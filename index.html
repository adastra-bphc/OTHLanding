  • Rules

    Welcome, intrepid adventurers! The Rebellion needs your help in its fight against the wicked Empire. We have intercepted a series of transmissions from our enemies - each of these transmissions is a puzzle that needs to be solved with a keyword. If you guess the correct keywords, we can use them to decipher future Imperial transmissions and save innocent lives. This is our most desperate hour. Help us - you are our only hope.
    1. You are on a quest to help the Rebellion! Your adventure will start with round 1 of Galactrix at 00:01 on 27th October 2023, and will end on 29th October, at 23:59.
    2. Fight solo or team up with up to 3 people in this cosmic crypt-hunt. Remember - you can only be a part of one team at a time! The event is open to everyone across the globe!
    3. Be sure to join our discord channel to communicate with the moderators and ask for clues.
    4. Decrypt your way through 5 stages of increasing difficulty in round 1. You must enter the correct key to move on to the next puzzle. You will get 5 tries per question to enter the correct key word, after which you will be locked out.
    5. Remember - the keyword will always be entirely in lowercase alphanumeric characters, with no spaces. If the answers you come up with have spaces or punctuation, omit them when entering your answer.
    Example: “American.Pie!” becomes “americanpie” when you enter the answer.
    6. You are free to use any resource available to you to help with your quest, including Google and ChatGPT.
    7. Beware of the time, because you are racing with all the other teams - the first ten teams to complete round 1 will gain free entry into round 2 of the challenge, which will be conducted during ATMOS ‘23. These top ten teams must compete in Atmos with the same members.
    8. In case less than ten teams complete round 1, points shall be allotted based on the number of rounds a team has completed and the amount of time they have taken.
    9. Teams who fail to qualify in Round 1 can still participate in Round 2 during ATMOS'23 by paying an entry fee.
    10. Any attempt to access levels you have not yet reached or any form of attack on the servers will result in instant disqualification.
    11. The organizer's decision is final in any and all cases.
    12. The Rebellion will reward you handsomely in the event that you succeed in the final adventure! The first three teams to succeed in their quests will win prizes from a pool of Rs. 15,000.

    Some Important Tips to remember

    1. Reverse searching images might help you in certain cases.
    2. The questions are open-ended, may have red herrings, cheeky hints and may lead you on wild goose chases. If you are getting nowhere with a certain approach, do consider that you might be on the wrong train of thought.
    3. On the other hand, it could be that the answer is ‘colour’ when you have been trying ‘color’. Check for alternate spelling, names and other such variations.
    4. Hints to the questions will be released on discord at the moderator’s discretion.
    5. The discord channel is the quickest way to reach the moderators of Galactrix. You may ask for hints on the forum.

  • About AD ASTRA   

    Wondering about a club that looks beyond the boundaries, into the stars? A club that aids you in viewing the heavens through a system of simple lenses? A club that expands your knowledge beyond the edges of the universe? Well then you have stumbled into the astronomy club of BITS Pilani Hyderabad Campus - Ad Astra.

    What do we do?

    Not only a bunch of enthusiastic stargazers, we integrate the technology we have on campus to take it to a next level. We analyze gigs of archived data on stars, hold talks in colleges regarding the heavenly bodies and do web dev for cool astro websites as you will see in the next few minutes.Oh! We have been featured on national news channels such as DD News and India Today for hosting a real time viewing of a world record mission, the soft landing of Chandrayaan-3. So you see, we love space, we are space, we are Ad Astra.

    About BITS Pilani

    Established in 1964 by Dr. G. D. Birla, Birla Institute of Technology and Science (BITS) is a brainchild of his grandfather and his lifelong dream that he made come true. The Hyderabad Campus, established in 2008, is the youngest of the four campuses, with over 5000 students and 450 faculty and staff members. Multiple clubs and chapters run by our enthusiastic and devoted students, our humongous fests and academic rigor have shaped our diverse campus culture over the years. Giving rise to hundreds of successful start-ups, BITS has always strived to light the fire of entrepreneurship and invention in every student of the university.
